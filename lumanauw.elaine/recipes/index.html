what you need

      ground coffee, or beans, or any kind of coffee :-)
      oatmilk (oatly preferrably, trust me)
      optional: honey or vanilla syrup or sugar

how to make

  1. Prepare your coffee. I have a coffee grinder and usually I grind to make 3 tbsp-ish coffee beans (usually more, depending on my mood). While doing that.. I heat up my water. Bring to a boil :-)
  2. Put coffee in a filter and the pour-over brewer, and put that on top of a glass. Slowly pour the boiling water, and wait for the mug to be half full-ish.
  3. grab your oatly! fill the rest of the cup with oatly, and add 2 tbsp of vanilla syrup *RECOMMENDED* or just sub that with honey.
  4. THANK ME LATER

what you need

      indomie noodles! *you can get them in the nearest asian grocery store :)
      eggs (optional)
      optional: sambal *extra spicy sauce

how to make

  1. Heat up water on a mid-sized pot and once it's boiling, add your Indomie noodles. Cook it for 3-5 minutes depending on how you like your noodles.
  2. Strain the noods and put in a bowl, add the seasoning packets.
  3. Fry up an egg, sunny side up.
  4. ready to go! THANK ME LATER

*nasi goreng is Bahasa for fried rice*

what you need

      2 eggs
      2 cups of day 2 rice
      1 clove of garlic, 1/4 onion, 5 small shallots
      ground beef (optional)
      sweet soy sauce (A MUST)
      soy sauce
      oyster sauce
      3 birds eye chili
      salt, pepper, sugar

how to make

  1. Dice these ingredients: garlic, onion, shallot, chilli. put them in a wok-pan with oil that's been heated.
  2. Once it starts smelling good, toss in your ground beef and wait for it to cook. PRO TIP: do NOT mix the beef. We want that yummy burnt texture in the beef.
  3. Once the beef is 3/4 cooked, make a well in the beef and add your eggs. Scramble them!
  4. Add rice, 4 tbsp sweet soy sauce, 1 tbsp of oyster sauce, 1 tsp soy sauce, a pinch of sugar, pepper, and salt.
  5. mix it all in... and you should be done!
  6. THANK ME LATER
